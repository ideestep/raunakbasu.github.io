Our lab investigates how neural circuits construct internal maps of the world and how those maps support flexible, goal-directed navigation and decision-making. We combine behavioral paradigms, electrophysiology, neural perturbation, and computational analysis to uncover underlying mechanisms.

Task-Relevant Cognitive Map Formation

Understanding how neural representations encode task-relevant environmental features beyond physical space.

Neural Basis of Spatial Decision-Making

Investigating how internal maps guide flexible decision strategies during navigation.

Distributed Map Coordination

Exploring communication between neural circuits supporting navigation and inference.
